About Us

UX/UI Design Club's mission is to provide Bowdoin students and its overall community a space for students to foster growth in UX/UI Design techniques, network with alumni and professors, and provide portfolio experience.

Established in 2023 at Bowdoin College, UX/UI Design Club was created to offer a platform for students looking to break into our world's current rising technological industry.


Club Goals

  1. Experience: We will work with professional programs such as Figma, practice together on different test cases and apply our skills to campus wide projects!
  2. Feedback: From alumni, related Bowdoin professors, and your own fellow members, you will receive feedback on each of your test cases!
  3. Networking: Members will have access to relevant alumni who work within UI/UX Design!
  4. Recommendations: A recommended class list from CS, Visual Arts, and DCS professors for an unofficial UI/UX track at Bowdoin!

What is UX/UI Design?

User Experience (UX): The process of planning the "experience" a person has when interacting with a product. They focus on the usage of the product and or service to be digitally/physically easy, logical and fun! UX Designers must find out WHO their users are (user persona) and create something that works well for them.

User Interface (UI): UI Designers design the aesthetics of a digital product, the look and feel. They focus on the visual touchpoints like color palette, typography, buttons, animations, etc. UI Designers are basically the graphic designers who aim to create a product interface that is aesthetic and easy to use!

Simply, it is the UI Designer's job to bring a UX Designer's ideas to life!


Why UX/UI Design?

  • It is an in-demand career where large industries and start-ups are looking for UX/UI Designers, especially for websites and apps!
  • The salary for UX/UI Designers start at around $77,108 for entry-level designers, and it only increases from there (varies from experience/location)!
  • UX/UI Design skills were cited as the top skills in 2021 by LinkedIn! Through experience, you can build lots of soft skills that are applicable to many other careers.
